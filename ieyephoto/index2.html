The Craft

Cyanotype Process

Cyanotype is one of the oldest photographic printing processes, invented by Sir John Herschel in 1842. It produces distinctive cyan-blue prints through a simple yet magical process involving iron salts and sunlight.

1

Prepare

Coat paper with light-sensitive iron solution and let dry in darkness

2

Compose

Arrange botanicals, objects, or negatives on the coated surface

3

Expose

Place under direct sunlight or UV light for several minutes

4

Develop

Rinse in water to reveal the Prussian blue image

The Artist

Luisa Rodrigues

Cyanotype Artist

I create one-of-a-kind cyanotype prints using the same sun-sensitive process invented in 1842. Each piece is made by hand, exposed under natural sunlight, and developed in water to reveal the signature Prussian blue tones.

My work explores the intersection of botanical forms, light, and time. I forage local plants, press flowers, and compose arrangements that become permanent impressions of fleeting natural beauty.
