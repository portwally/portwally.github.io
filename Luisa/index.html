THE CRAFT

Cyanotype process

Cyanotype is one of the oldest photographic printing processes, invented by Sir John Herschel in 1842. It produces distinctive cyan-blue prints through a simple yet magical process involving iron salts and sunlight.

1

PREPARE

Coat paper with light-sensitive iron solution and let dry in darkness

2

COMPOSE

Arrange botanicals, objects, or negatives on the coated surface

3

EXPOSE

Place under direct sunlight or UV light for several minutes

4

DEVELOPE

Rinse in water to reveal the Prussian blue image

THE ARTIST

Luísa Rodrigues

Based in Portugal, I've been exploring the cyanotype process for over a decade, drawn by its meditative nature and the beautiful unpredictability of each print. Working with sunlight, chemistry, and botanical specimens, I create one-of-a-kind artworks that capture the delicate beauty of the natural world in shades of Prussian blue.

My work is inspired by the intersection of science and art — the same curiosity that led Anna Atkins to create the first photographic book of botanical specimens using this very technique in 1843.

As a Climate Aware Photographer, I integrate low‑carbon practices into my work and critically reflect on the social and environmental impact of images.
