Inside the app

A full screenshot studio. Nothing extra.

Drag-drop import, painterly backgrounds, typography you actually want to use, and per-app presets you apply across every locale with one click.

⤓

Drop. Render. Ship.

Drop any PNG, JPG or HEIC onto the canvas — Screen2Store composites it onto the background instantly, at the exact target resolution. Drag the screenshot anywhere; centre snap-guides keep it perfectly aligned.

◐

Backgrounds, three ways

Solid colours, two-stop gradients (twelve curated presets), or images. Pick from the macOS system wallpapers built in, or import your own.

A

Real typography

Full system font picker, weight, color, alignment, line spacing, drop shadow. Text positions itself above, below or as overlay.

🌐

Every language

Add as many locales as you ship. Each locale gets its own caption per slot — switch in the sidebar, edit, export. Folders organised en-US/, de-DE/…

★

Save as preset

Background plus type style equals one preset. Apply it to every screen of an app, or override on a single slot when you need to.

⤴

Pixel-perfect export

Pick a folder. Screen2Store writes <App>/<locale>/<store>/<device>/NN.png — every PNG at exactly the size that store requires, ready to drag straight into App Store Connect or Play Console.

⌘

One library, many apps

Add as many apps as you maintain. Each one keeps its own enabled device sizes, locales, presets and screen slots. Switch between them from the sidebar.

Flow

Add an app. Drop screenshots. Export.

No subscriptions, no template marketplace, no learning curve. Three steps you can stop at any time.

Add your app

Click + Add App in the sidebar. Pick which device sizes you need from the Devices… picker — iPhone, iPad, Mac, Play phone, Play tablet, feature graphic.

Drop & design

Drag your raw screenshots onto the canvas. Set the background, type a caption, choose your font. Save the combination as a preset and apply it to every screen with one click.

Export

Hit Export, pick a folder. PNGs land in tidy per-locale, per-device subfolders at the exact pixel size each store wants — ready to upload.
